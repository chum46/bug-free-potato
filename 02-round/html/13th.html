Chaminda

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Narrative was clear and expertly supported. The people they interviewed gave great perspective and context. Bryan Stevenson is someone I really admire and I hang on every word he says. I love how they showed the through-line to the present day version of the cycle of oppression. Showing how cryptically written laws allow for racist enforcement was eye-opening. If ALEC wrote these laws with racist intentions did those who passed them know? Or were they just cashing checks?

Andy

⭐⭐⭐⭐1/2

Overall a fair presentation of a really difficult topic. I think the director's political leanings were evident, but not in a way that was overly confrontational towards those on the right side aisle. Recognizing this as an American problem leaves space for bipartisan solutions.
